PC Parts: the real nerdy shit

When you're looking at a laptop you wanna buy online, the specifications or specs section will give you all the info you need about the product. If you don't wanna get into the techy descriptions of each of the parts just skip the first paragraphs of each section (ಠ_ಠ)

Processor (CPU)

Think of it as the brain and heart of your computer. You might see the words cores and threads to describe the strength of the processor. In general for each of these, the more the better. More cores let you overload the computer with more tasks while more threads move information faster. This leads to higher clock speeds, which are also a good thing.

CPU identifier benchmarks
Level Intel AMD Silicon (Apple)
Low Core i3 Ryzen 3 --
Mid-Range
(recommended bare minimum)		 Core i5 Ryzen 5 --

Note: This table is a general rule of thumb, NOT a definitive hierarchy. One outlier is a certain i5-1300 series chip that's even more powerful than some i9's. And tbh the M2 chip is only slightly better than the M1 I just didn't wanna make more table rows

It's also worth paying attention to the generation of the CPU, which can be found as the first two numbers following the hyphen in Intel chips or the first number following the AMD identifier.

Intel Core i7-1200H, 12th gen
AMD Ryzen 7 6800H, 6th gen

As usual, the higher the generation the better. I highly recommend gen 11 and above for Intel and gen 5 and above for AMD.

Storage

Now we need somewhere to store all of those Word Docs, PDFs, study guides, course syllabuses (syllabi?), music and streaming apps, unauthorized lecture recordings, minecraft worlds, and pro fortnite gaming clips that you'll accumulate over the next couple years. All this data is stored in a hard drive. In laptops today you'll prob only find the only storage option is an SSD, or solid state drive. The best hard drives offer the fastest read and write speeds, which are measures of how quickly data is accessed and saved (in Mb/s).

SSD Types
Level Type Read, Write (Mb/s)
Decent SATA 550, 550
Solid...get it? NVMe with PCIe 3.0 3500, 3500
Goated NVMe with PCIe 4.0 7000, 6500
Victory Royale NVMe with PCIe 5.0 12500, 10000

Long story short, look for NVMe and PCIe and everything will load and download quickly.

You also should think about how much storage you actually need. I recommend at least 256 GB to last you through the next couple years. If you plan on getting a Mac, upgrading to 512 GB will actually make your storage even faster because of the dual-channels, which I don't feel like explaining. Remember that some laptops have storage u can upgrade later on while others don't.

Memory (RAM)

MEMORY AND STORAGE ARE NOT THE SAME... so here's the difference: random access memory, or RAM is basically a much smaller and immensely quicker component that holds temporary data for easy access. RAM is the kitchen countertop where you assemble ur sandwich while storage is the fridge or pantry storing all the ingredients. The amount, type, and frequency of RAM are the important parts. The amount of memory lets u keep 300 tabs open in the background, and the type and frequency make all the data move faster.

RAM types
Level Type Speed Range (Mhz)
Obsolete DDR3 and under slow asl
Solid DDR4 2133-3200
Goated DDR5 3200-6400

The amount of RAM you have lets you do a lot more all at once. 8 GB is a standard minimum, but 16 GB is the way to go if you're feelin' it now, mr krabs. Personally I think 32 GB is straight up unnecessary and anything more than that is overkill.

Graphics

If you have no idea what a graphics card is, don't worry, you prob don't need to, your integrated graphics will be just fine for ur college endeavors. But you fs won't be able to run Fortnite smoothly on your new laptop. If you aren't getting a Mac and want Fortnite to run a little more smoothly (or know you'll be doing something with 3D modeling, photo/video editing, or digital art), look for the words Nvidia RTX (or GTX on the lower end), or the words Radeon RX. These are graphics processing units, or GPUs. As usual, the higher the numbers that follow, the better...most of the time
But if you are getting a Mac, the more cores the better. And you can prob run Fortnite on it at a decent graphics level and framerate.
